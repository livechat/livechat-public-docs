# Introduction

[Developer Console](https://platform.text.com/console/) allows you not only to create [LiveChat Apps](/getting-started/livechat-apps), but also to develop integrations for <a href="https://www.helpdesk.com/" target="_blank">HelpDesk</a> and publish them on the <a href="https://www.helpdesk.com/marketplace/" target="_blank">HelpDesk Marketplace</a>.

Connect your HelpDesk to an external tool or service, extend HelpDesk user interface, or automate various tasks. Configure different functionalities, or create your own custom actions for agents in HelpDesk.

# Use cases

HelpDesk provides developers with multiple tools which allow your app to do many different things, for example:

- Integrate with other products and services
- Extend HelpDesk user interfaces
- Create your own custom actions for agents in HelpDesk
- Automate tasks with webhooks
- Let users sign in to your service via [Accounts SDK](/authorization/sign-in-with-livechat#accounts-sdk)

# App locations in HelpDesk

Your app can extend several areas of the HelpDesk App interface:

- **Ticket Details**, by adding a new tab with your web content or a new widget in the default Ticket Details tab
- **Fullscreen**, by adding a new, fullscreen section with your web content
- **App Settings**, by adding a page with configuration settings for your app

## Ticket Details

Apps in the Ticket Details tabs are the most common way to extend the HelpDesk UI. They are displayed in the right sidebar of the HelpDesk App when browsing Tickets.

The main menu is a place where you can add a new, fullscreen section with your web content. Fullscreen apps are located in the navigation bar on the left, and they open maximized in the HelpDesk App.

## App Settings

The App Settings is a place where you can add a page with configuration settings for your app. Usually, developers use this placement to handle the cases where:

- Users need to enter an API key or other credentials for the 3rd party service.
- Admins can extend or restrict app permissions for agents.
- Users can customize app settings per group.
- Triggering [app authorization](/authorization/sign-in-with-livechat), especially if your app doesn't have a Ticket Details widget.

### Step 2 Adding HelpDesk Widgets block

You can form your app using components called building blocks, which serve different purposes. To learn more about building blocks, go to [App guides](/getting-started/app-guides).

In this guide, we will use the HelpDesk Widgets Building Block. Widgets are web apps that can interact and communicate with the HelpDesk App. They are loaded inside the HelpDesk App interface.

<img
  alt="Creating HelpDesk apps building blocks"
  src="/images/getting-started/helpdesk-apps/livechat-helpdesk-apps-building-blocks.png"
  width="1476px"
  height="771px"
/>

To add a new Widget to your application, you need to follow these steps:

1. Go to the **Building Blocks** tab.
2. Add a new HelpDesk Widgets block by clicking the **+Add building block** button and then choosing **HelpDesk Widgets**.
3. Enter the **Widget source URL**, which is the address of your application. If it's not ready yet, you can use our [sample apps](/extending-agent-app/agent-app-sdk#sample-apps) to play around.
4. Now, go to the **Widget placement** and choose one of the options. It allows you to decide where exactly in the HelDesk App you want to display your integration. We've chosen the **Fullscreen App** option in our example. In the HelpDesk App, full screen apps are located in the navigation bar on the left, and they open maximized in the HelpDesk App. [Learn more...](/extending-agent-app#main-menu-fullscreen-apps)
5. The **Opening state** section isn't required and works only for the **Ticket Details** widget placement. It allows you to add components to your application and see a live preview of what the app will look like in the **Opening state preview**. [Learn more...](/app-guides/helpdesk-widgets#building-block-configuration).
6. Click the **Create** button and enjoy your app's first building block.

### Step 3 Configuring Display details

Now, it is time for the **Display details** tab that allows you to configure your app's short description and add an icon for better visibility in the HelpDesk App.

Follow the steps below to add a description and icon:

1. Go to the **Display details** tab.
2. In the **Short description** section, add a one-sentence description of your app to make your users aware of what your app does. You can change the description later.
3. Add an icon for your app. It'll be displayed on the Marketplace and in the HelpDesk App. You can drag and drop your image, browse your file manager for it, or generate a random icon.

Before you are able to publish your application on the Marketplace, you need to test it first to ensure that no bugs or errors appear.

To do that, you need to install your app privately on your license by going to the **Private installation** tab and clicking the **Install app** button.
